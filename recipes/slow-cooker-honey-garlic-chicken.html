Slow Cooker Honey Garlic Chicken

Description

Save the white styrofoam container for a more desperate time—today we’re making easy Slow Cooker Honey Garlic Chicken. Its sweet, sticky sauce has all the allure of your favorite takeout but is made of healthy, natural ingredients. The recipe is only eight ingredients (nine if you are a stickler and count the rice), and if you cook Asian food regularly (meaning you own soy sauce), you have all of them in your pantry right now.

Ingredients

  • 700g boneless, skinless chicken thighs or chicken breasts
  • ⅓ cup low-sodium soy sauce
  • ⅓ cup honey
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons chili paste sambal oelek, sriracha, or hot sauce of choice
  • 4 cloves garlic minced
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 2 cups of rice

Steps

  1. Place the chicken in the bottom of a 4- to 6-quart slow cooker.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, honey, tomato paste, chili paste, garlic, and rice vinegar. Pour over the chicken.
  3. Cover and cook on low for 4 to 5 hours or high for 2 to 3 hours, until the chicken reaches an internal temperature of 165 degrees F.
  4. Remove the chicken to a plate. In a small bowl, whisk together the cornstarch with 2 tablespoons cold water. Whisk into the slow cooker sauce.
  5. Re-cover and cook on high for 20 minutes, until the sauce thickens.
  6. Return the chicken to the slow cooker and toss with the sauce. Serve hot over rice.
